Types of Fractions

  • Proper Fractions: Numerator is less than the denominator (e.g., ¾).
  • Improper Fractions: Numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator (e.g., 5/4).
  • Mixed Numbers: A whole number combined with a proper fraction (e.g., 1 ¼).

Properties of Fractions

Addition and Subtraction

To add or subtract fractions, find a common denominator.

Multiplication and Division

To multiply fractions, multiply the numerators and denominators. To divide, multiply by the reciprocal.

Daily Usage of Fractions

  • Cooking: Using fractions in recipes (e.g., ½ cup of sugar).
  • Finance: Understanding discounts (e.g., 25% off means paying ¾ of the original price).
  • Construction: Measuring lengths (e.g., cutting wood into ⅓ pieces).
